Why Neuropsych Now?

Not convinced that easy access to the most current information is important? Consider this quote from Alexander & Roberts (1998):

“The field of clinical psychology is diverse and informational material continually becomes available in a multitude of places. Because the half-life of psychological knowledge is brief, the need for updating and accessing resources is nearly constant for both researchers and practitioners alike.”[1]

W. Howard Buddin Jr., Ph.D.

Dr. Buddin works at the University of Kansas School of Medicine-Wichita. His research interests include statistics, research methodology, and neurocognitive decline in adults.

S. Marc Testa, Ph.D.

Dr. Testa works at the Baltimore VA Medical Center and is part-time faculty at Johns Hopkins University. His research includes cognitive functioning among people with epilepsy and developing new approaches to measuring cognitive functioning.
